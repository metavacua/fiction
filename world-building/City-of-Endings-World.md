# World-Building: The City of Endings

This document outlines the key locations, concepts, and cultural norms of the setting for "City of Endings."

## Core Concepts

*   **The Narrative City:** The city is not just a physical place but a living, semi-sentient process. It "accrues stories in layers" and is constantly "mutating" to fit the needs of its residents. Its primary obsession is with **narrative, closure, and endings.**
*   **Narrative Machinery:** A tangible but unseen force that governs the city. It can be felt "grinding just below the threshold of perception." The city's infrastructure and culture are built to serve this machinery, ensuring every event has a satisfying narrative arc.
*   **Myth-Powered:** The city "runs on myth." Its logic is not entirely rational but based on stories, symbols, and archetypes.
*   **Iterations & Repetition:** The world, and the people in it, repeat themselves in "variations." The city keeps a record of these past iterations, as seen in the Memory Palace's registry of Maya Patel's previous lives.

## Key Institutions

*   **The Institute:** The primary academic and bureaucratic power in the city.
    *   **Purpose:** To study and control the city's narrative flow. It analyzes "mythological thinking" and is obsessed with ensuring "closure" for all events.
    *   **Departments:** Includes the **Department of Narrative Studies** (Morgan's department) and employs "Quants" like Maya to analyze data.
    *   **Architecture:** The main building is an optical illusion, appearing as a fortress, cathedral, or question mark from different angles.
    *   **Ideology:** Represents a rigid, top-down approach to storytelling, which Maya finds stifling.

## Notable Locations & Landmarks

*   **Maya's Quarters (Converted Memory-Archive):** A former library turned into housing for researchers. Its flaws (high ceilings, echoing rooms, shrieking plumbing) provide comfort to Maya, as it feels "unfinished."
*   **The Placeholder:** A prominent statue of a woman holding her own head, her features out of focus. A place of contemplation for Maya.
*   **Market of Borrowed Scenes:** A market where vendors sell snippets of other people's memories, which are considered a social necessity for belonging.
*   **Epitaph Fountain:** A fountain whose jets of water spell out different names at random.
*   **Contradiction Steps:** A set of stairs where each riser is cut at a different, unpredictable angle.
*   **The Memory Palace (Building 4.7.2.1):** An unmarked building that serves as a collective memory bank for the city. Mirrors on the walls display the "most significant moment" of every person who has ever lived there. It contains a registry of all inhabitants and their past iterations.
*   **The Narrative Checkpoint:** The formal boundary for leaving the city. It is not a physical barrier but a ritualistic space with a dais, two chairs, and a scribe who records all departures and arrivals.
*   **The Bridge of Codified Departures:** An old, sagging bridge that leads to the Narrative Checkpoint. A monument at the end lists the names of everyone who has left by that route, with annotations like 'Returned' or 'Stayed.'

## Cultural Norms & Phenomena

*   **Narrative Satisfaction Rituals:** The city spends a significant portion of its resources on rituals to ensure closure, such as reconciliation dinners, public apologies, and the annual burning of obsolete legislation.
*   **Improvised Greetings:** Handshakes and other standard greetings have been replaced by a requirement to improvise new ones.
*   **Narrative Seeds:** Tiny "pips of story" that are sown on the sidewalks, designed to "take root in passing minds."
*   **The Iteration Parade:** A festival where citizens dress up as alternate versions of themselves.
*   **Leaving as a Formal Process:** Departing the city is a structured, witnessed event, complete with scribes and registries. It is a core part of the city's identity.
