# Creative Provenance Ontology

This document defines a simple ontology for tracking the provenance of creative work generated in a human-AI collaborative process. The goal is to clearly delineate between human-authored contributions (copyrightable) and AI-generated suggestions (not copyrightable).

## Core Concepts

The model is built around a sequence of "Creative Actions." Each action has a timestamp, an actor (human or AI), and content.

### 1. `AuthorPrompt`

*   **Description:** A specific instruction or piece of text written by the human author to guide the AI. This is the initial seed for a generation cycle.
*   **Actor:** Human
*   **Copyright Status:** **Copyrightable.** This is an original expression by the author.

### 2. `AIGeneratedSuggestion`

*   **Description:** A piece of text generated by the AI based on an `AuthorPrompt`. These are considered raw material and are not yet part of the final work. A single prompt may result in multiple, distinct suggestions.
*   **Actor:** AI
*   **Copyright Status:** **Not Copyrightable.** This is the direct output of the machine.

### 3. `AuthorSelection`

*   **Description:** The act of the human author choosing one or more `AIGeneratedSuggestion`(s) to incorporate into the work. This action signifies creative judgment.
*   **Actor:** Human
*   **Copyright Status:** **Copyrightable Act.** The selection itself is a creative choice that confers authorship over the *arrangement* of the selected text.

### 4. `AuthorEdit`

*   **Description:** The modification of a selected `AIGeneratedSuggestion` by the human author. This can range from fixing a typo to rewriting entire sentences. This is a clear act of creative authorship.
*   **Actor:** Human
*   **Copyright Status:** **Copyrightable.** The resulting text is a derivative work created by the author, and the edits themselves are original expression.

## Workflow Example

A typical workflow would follow this sequence:

1.  A human author writes an **`AuthorPrompt`**.
2.  The AI generates several **`AIGeneratedSuggestion`**s.
3.  The author performs an **`AuthorSelection`** on one of the suggestions.
4.  The author makes several **`AuthorEdit`**s to the selected text, transforming it into the final version that is added to the manuscript.

By tracking this chain of events, we can create a clear audit trail for the provenance of every word in the final manuscript, distinguishing the author's copyrightable contributions from the AI's non-copyrightable suggestions.
